## Domain Knowledge Added This Session

**610 events — actuarial framing:** The 610 "adverse events" are location-month-peril threshold exceedances across 6 locations and 3 peril types over 26 years (1,872 total records). The individually defensible actuarial numbers are the per-peril trigger rates: drought 12%, flood 9.3%, crop failure 6.2%. The aggregate 32.6% rate is a mathematical artefact of summing 4 perils across 6 locations, not evidence of threshold miscalibration. Framing each event as "one farmer crisis" does not survive technical scrutiny.

**Loss ratio — Morogoro vs 6-location:** The 75% loss ratio is Morogoro pilot calibration. The $1,590/year payout aggregate spans all 6 training locations. Dividing these two different-scale figures gives ~80% — a multi-location artefact. Do not change the 75% figure; explain the scale mismatch with the clarifying note now in all three parametric product docs.

**Reserve sizing + correlation clustering:** The $150,000 reserve is conservative by design — it accounts for the fact that La Niña/IOD episodes can drive simultaneous threshold breaches across multiple locations in the same season, causing actual annual payouts to cluster rather than distributing evenly at the modelled average. The 1.67× worst-case coverage is the buffer against this clustering risk.
